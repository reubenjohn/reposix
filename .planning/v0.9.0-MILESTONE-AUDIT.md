# v0.9.0 Milestone Audit

## Verdict

**`status: tech_debt`** — every milestone-level engineering success criterion that
can be verified inside the dev environment is **passed**. Two items carry forward
to v0.10.0 (or later) intentionally:

1. **Helper hardcodes `SimBackend`** (carry-forward tech debt from Phase 32).
   The `stateless-connect` handler in `crates/reposix-remote/src/main.rs`
   currently routes every backend to `SimBackend`. URL-scheme dispatch
   (`github::`, `confluence::`, `jira::`) was deferred when Phase 32 shipped —
   noted in `32-VERIFICATION.md` line 107, `32-SUMMARY.md` lines 110–134, and
   re-flagged in `35-RESEARCH.md` line 21, `35-03-PLAN.md` line 29, and
   `35-03-SUMMARY.md` line 79. Phase 35 added soft-init assertions for real
   backends but did not address the helper-side dispatch. **This is the
   tech-debt item that tilts the verdict from `passed` to `tech_debt`.**
2. **Real-backend integration jobs `pending-secrets`** (Phase 35 / Phase 36
   carry-forward). The CI infrastructure (`integration-contract-{confluence,
   github, jira}-v09`) and the gated cargo tests (`agent_flow_real`) are wired;
   they remain `pending-secrets` until secret packs decrypt in CI. **This is a
   human gate, not a gap in the engineering work** — Phase 35 ships the test
   infrastructure, owner ships the secrets.

The lifecycle continues through Stage 2 (complete-milestone) and Stage 3
(cleanup). The helper-hardcoded-SimBackend item is captured in v0.10.0 forward
plan as a milestone-level concern and surfaces below in §5.

## 5. Tech-debt — helper hardcodes SimBackend

**Symptom.** `crates/reposix-remote/src/main.rs` instantiates `SimBackend` for
every `reposix init` URL today, regardless of whether the user provided
`github::owner/repo`, `confluence::space`, or `jira::PROJ`. Phase 35 made
`reposix init` *config* the right remote URL (e.g.
`reposix::https://api.github.com/projects/<owner>/<repo>`), but the helper
ignores the URL host and routes everything to `SimBackend`.

**Trail of evidence.**

- `32-VERIFICATION.md:107` — "backend name hardcoded to `\"sim\"` pending Phase 35
  URL parsing".
- `32-SUMMARY.md:110–115` — "Backend name hardcoded to `\"sim\"`: v0.9.0 is
  sim-only".
- `32-SUMMARY.md:131–134` — explicit hand-off note: "Phase 35 (`reposix init`
  + real backends): derive `backend_name` from `spec.origin` host (github,
  confluence, jira, sim). The `State.backend_name` field is the seam."
- `35-RESEARCH.md:21` — "the helper currently hardcodes `SimBackend`… the helper
  itself remains sim-only until a future phase teaches it real backends".
- `35-03-PLAN.md:29` — Phase 35 explicitly accepts a "soft assertion" on real
  backends (test verifies init succeeds + URL is correct, not that fetch
  works).
- `35-03-SUMMARY.md:79` — Phase 35 enumerates "Helper multi-backend dispatch"
  as a follow-up.

**Why it didn't block the milestone.**

- The dark-factory test infrastructure for sim **does** prove the architecture
  end-to-end (Phase 35-02 + Phase 36 skill).
- The real-backend smoke tests assert what they CAN assert without the helper
  upgrade: init configures the right URL, env vars are read, skip_if_no_env!
  gating is honest.
- Phase 36's `pending-secrets` CI status is a layered gate — once the helper
  multi-backend dispatch lands, the existing CI matrix will pick it up.

**Why this still tilts the audit to `tech_debt`, not `passed`.**

- ARCH-12's "agent UX = pure git, zero in-context learning" claim cannot be
  end-to-end-proven against a real backend until the helper dispatches by URL
  host. We have the *infrastructure* but the *transport* still terminates at
  SimBackend. Calling the milestone `passed` would understate this.
- The user explicitly authorized "tech_debt" as the right verdict for this
  exact carry-forward in the lifecycle prompt.

**Carry-forward into v0.10.0.**

- v0.10.0's "Docs & Narrative Shine" milestone (Phases 40–45 in
  `.planning/research/v0.10.0-post-pivot/milestone-plan.md`) is doc-focused,
  not transport-focused. The helper-multi-backend-dispatch fix should land as
  a Phase ~36.1 hotfix or as a v0.11.0 ("Performance & Sales Assets") prereq —
  the v0.11.0 benchmark numbers can't be honest without the helper actually
  talking to real backends.
- Recommendation: track as a backlog item before v0.10.0 starts; resolution
  before any v0.11.0 benchmark commits.
