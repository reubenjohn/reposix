Invocation pattern (run for EACH file or small batch):
```bash
REVIEW_DIR=$(mktemp -d /tmp/doc-clarity-review-XXXXXX)
DEFAULT_PROMPT='You are reviewing one or more files in complete isolation. You have NOT seen the repo, the codebase, or any other files. Do not follow any links. Do not request additional context. Do not use any tools. Read only what is provided.

Your job: give honest, unbiased feedback on the document clarity for a reader arriving cold.

## Friction Points
Specific sentences, paragraphs, or terms that are confusing, ambiguous, or require outside knowledge not given in this document.

## Unanswered Questions
What do you still not understand after reading this? What would you need to know before acting on this document?

## Over-Explained
What sections repeat themselves, state the obvious, or could be cut without losing meaning?

## Under-Explained
What critical concepts are mentioned but not explained? What would a first-time reader need to look up?

## Missing References
What links or "see also" pointers are clearly absent?

## Verdict
In 2 sentences: would an agent landing on these files cold understand what the system does and what to do next?
Rate: CLEAR / NEEDS WORK / CONFUSING'

echo "$DEFAULT_PROMPT" > "$REVIEW_DIR/_prompt.md"
cp <file> "$REVIEW_DIR/$(basename <file>)"
claude -p "$(cat $REVIEW_DIR/_prompt.md)" "$REVIEW_DIR/$(basename <file>)" 2>&1 | tee "$REVIEW_DIR/_feedback_$(basename <file> .md).md"
```
</context>
